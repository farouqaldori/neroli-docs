title: "Privacy"
description: "How to think about code, prompts, providers, and cloud handoff."
---

Neroli is local-first: your repositories are opened from your Mac, and review
happens before you merge. The data a provider sees depends on the provider, the
prompt, attached files, terminal commands, and whether you intentionally use
cloud handoff.

## Local providers

Local provider CLIs may still send prompts, files, and command context to their
own services. Check the provider's policy and your account settings before
including sensitive material.

## Cloud handoff

When cloud handoff is used, Neroli transfers the state needed to continue the
session. Do not move work to cloud if it depends on secrets or files that should
stay local.

## Good habits

- Attach the smallest context that solves the task.
- Keep secrets out of prompts and committed files.
- Review diffs for accidental local files.
- Use separate sessions for sensitive experiments.

<Info>
  This page is operational guidance, not a legal privacy policy.
